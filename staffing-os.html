02 · why this isn't Bullhorn + Copilot

Three things they structurally can't do.

Bullhorn is a firm-owned silo. Copilot is an assistant on your tenant. Both only know what was typed or said — and neither verifies anything.

i

Give the candidate a seat

Copilot can update a record from a meeting. It can't make the candidate keep their data current in the 90 days between touches. Only the candidate can — so in their world, rot is permanent.

→ the candidate owns and maintains it
ii

Verify, not just store

Bullhorn stores claims; Copilot summarizes them. Neither tests a skill, confirms employment, or live-confirms availability. A "match" is keyword guesswork.

→ every claim verified at the source
iii

Cross the firm boundary

A single-tenant ATS and a tenant-bound Copilot can never be a network. A candidate-owned, consented profile is portable across every firm that earns access.

→ a living, consented talent network
